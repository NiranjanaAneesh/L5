Facts About Tigers

Size: Tigers are the largest big cats in the wild. Male tigers can grow up to 3.5 meters long and weigh up to 250 kilograms, while females can grow up to 3 meters long and weigh up to 170 kilograms. Habitat: Tigers live in tropical, subtropical, and temperate regions of Asia, including India, Nepal, northeastern China, and eastern Russia. Diet: Tigers eat large prey like wild pigs and deer. Stripes: Each tiger has unique stripes. Communication: Tigers can communicate using their ears and have a wide range of vocalizations. Swimming: Tigers are excellent swimmers and enjoy spending time in water. They often bathe in rivers to cool off. Cubs: Tiger cubs are born blind and weigh between 1.75 and 3.5 pounds. Their eyes open after about six to twelve days. Lifespan: In the wild, tigers typically live around 11 years. Endangered: Tigers are endangered, and an average of two tigers are seized from traffickers every week. Roaring: Tigers can roar, but they don't purr. Their roar is a series of short snarls that can be heard from almost two kilometers away. Breeding: There isn't a fixed breeding season for tigers, but mating most commonly occurs in the winter.
